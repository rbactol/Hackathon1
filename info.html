About Us

Background

The COVID-19 pandemic has exacerbated education inequalities, and Support Our Students (SOS) counters this growing inequality gap by bringing the Ramsey County community together to share resources and make sure all students can prosper. Educating the next generation is the most powerful way to empower our community, and we can overcome the obstacles presented by this pandemic by collaborating. SOS facilitates this collaboration by employing elements of a sharing economy to match underused assets and available services with students in need. Website description

SOS is a crowdsourced community resource that was created in response to the COVID-19 pandemic and the educational inequalities it produced. This map-based website is populated with data by users, connecting available technology and information to students who lack the resources to successfully adapt to online classes.

About us

SOS was created during a Hack-a-Thon organized by Hack The Gap and sponsored by Ramsey County. The design of this project will allow it to expand with time as it reaches a greater audience. Success will be measured by the quantity of resources shared, and we expect that this will exponentially increase overtime as more community members gain access to our site. SOS seeks to add value to Ramsey County by employing sustainable solutions for the community, by the community. By recycling old technology and providing information on shared resources, the county will reduce waste and reep the benefits of a sharing economy. Team members are Roman Bactol, Imane Chantri, Willow Fortunoff, Hayley Hadges, and Isabella Valdivia.
